Organic Farming

Organic farming is an agricultural production approach that prioritizes natural inputs and ecological processes above artificial chemicals and genetically modified organisms (GMOs).


Composting, crop rotation, green manures, and biological pest management are the tools that organic farmers use to control pests and diseases and preserve soil fertility. Organic farming lowers greenhouse gas emissions, conserves biodiversity, and safeguards soil and water quality by forgoing synthetic fertilizers and pesticides.


Organic certification standards guarantee honesty and openness in the production of organic food, giving customers peace of mind that goods are made in compliance with stringent organic guidelines. In addition to promoting rural development and food sovereignty, organic farming provides farmers with financial opportunities through premium prices, market differentiation, and access to niche markets.
