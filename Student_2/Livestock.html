Sustainable Livestock Management

Practices that maximize resource utilization in livestock production systems, reduce environmental impact, and advance animal welfare are all included in sustainable livestock management. It places a strong emphasis on environmental sustainability, effective resource use, and animal health and welfare.


In order to allow animals to express their natural behaviors and have access to outside spaces, sustainable livestock systems place a higher priority on pasture-based and mixed farming systems than on intense confinement operations. Integrated crop-livestock systems, rotational grazing, and silvopasture are a few examples of sustainable livestock management techniques that boost biodiversity, replenish soil, and store carbon.


Sustainable livestock management minimizes greenhouse gas emissions, conserves water resources, and lessens environmental pollution by minimizing dependency on feedlot operations and synthetic inputs. Furthermore, sustainable livestock production supports rural livelihoods, cultural heritage, and food security. This is especially true for marginalized and indigenous groups who depend on livestock for social cohesion, money, and food.
